# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary users are technical outbound and RevOps teams at SMBs, especially in WhatsApp-first markets, who currently rely on brittle combinations of CRM, automation tools, providers, and spreadsheets. They are comfortable with APIs, self-hosting, and opinionated tools, and they evaluate products with both technical and commercial scrutiny.

Secondary users include founders, operators, agencies, and OSS-minded buyers comparing Kizunu against closed sales-engagement tools. They need to understand quickly that Kizunu is extensible, credible, and different from generic SaaS software.

## Product Purpose

Kizunu is an open-source sales engagement engine for multi-channel outbound cadences with reply-stop and pluggable providers. It exists to replace fragile CRM plus automation glue with a channel-agnostic, API-first system that technical teams can self-host, adapt, and extend.

The product should make a strong first impression as infrastructure with taste: rigorous enough for operators, flexible enough for builders, and distinctive enough to feel like a category alternative rather than another sales tool clone. Success means teams can trust the engine to run real outbound workflows while clearly seeing the value of the plugin system and open-core model.

## Brand Personality

Experimental, creative, tech AI.

The tone should feel like an experimental creative studio applied to infrastructure software: opinionated, research-driven, future-facing, and visually curious. It should signal optimism about AI and new tooling without sounding hype-driven or generic. The brand should feel authored, not templated, and should reward technical audiences who notice detail, intent, and craft.

## Anti-references

Avoid common SaaS defaults, common AI defaults, and generic B2B product polish. This should not look like a CRM template, a safe startup landing page, a sterile enterprise dashboard, or an "AI workflow" brand assembled from familiar gradients, soft cards, and vague claims.

Avoid visual and verbal patterns that make Kizunu feel interchangeable with closed sales tools or fashionable-but-empty AI products. The product should not borrow the tone of bland productivity software, venture-backed landing-page kits, or predictable RevOps software aesthetics.

## Design Principles

1. Make infrastructure feel authored. Treat the brand as a point of view, not packaging pasted onto a tool.
2. Prove the thesis through specificity. Show concrete workflows, exact system behavior, and real extensibility instead of abstract promises.
3. Use experimentation as research. Bold choices should communicate curiosity and technical ambition, not novelty for its own sake.
4. Stay open-source in posture, not just licensing. The experience should feel transparent, adaptable, and builder-friendly.
5. Reject category camouflage. Every important surface should feel meaningfully distinct from default SaaS and default AI design patterns.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Design ambition leads, but functional clarity still matters. Core product flows should remain keyboard-friendly, readable, and navigable under real working conditions.

Target pragmatic accessibility on functional surfaces: strong contrast for essential text and controls, reduced-motion consideration when motion could interfere with use, and support for users who rely on keyboard navigation. Brand-led expression can push harder visually, but not at the cost of basic comprehension or task completion.
